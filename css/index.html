Food Service Application

Built using Java, this desktop application was inspired by my personal struggles as a freshman. First Year students now do not need to walk to each residence cafeteria to know what's on the menu. The app collects information for each restaurant in each residence cafeteria. There is also a page that webscrapes to display the timings of the residence cafeterias across UBC.

Text-to-Speech Converter

Built in Android Studio, this app was built at NwHacks 2019. My team had the idea of combining this app with Google's image-to-text converter to help visually-impaired individuals scan their surroundings using their phone. A visually-impaired individual can take a picture of their surroundings using shortcuts on their phone, and the app will help describe objects around them. This would help prevent injury and increase accessibility.

UBC Course Data Visualizer

Built in HTML, CSS and JavaScript, this web application serves its purpose by offering UBC students a variety of course data, and a visualizer to make it more intuitive. Currently, students use two different portals to access aggregate course data and personal course data. This application helps transport the aggregated course data onto Canvas, which has a better User interface and is more frequently used for academic purposes. This application was built during Learning Analytics Hackathon 2019

BizHacks 2020 (Finalists)

I helped my team build a working prototype for a website that facilitates a loyalty program for Best Buy. Customers who purchase certain products periodically such as Water filters can now set up a subscription. This increases customer loyalty for Best Buy, and reduces hassle for the customer to make purchases every time. We were selected as finalists out of 53 participating teams. The website was built using node.js, HTML and CSS

Imprint 2020

I contributed to my team's suggestions for WildCoast Fruits to add sales channels to their current product portfolio. WildCoast is a market leader in terms of quality and does not compete in price. Therefore, we focused on creating a brand image of healthy eating, and suggested that WildCoast pursue offices to purchase their products in wholesale as snacks for their employees. Our marketing campaign involved contacting small office spaces for testing and feedback, and then scaling to that larger spaces.
